# Chapter 4 | The EdTech x AI Industry Landscape

## One-Sentence Takeaway

The core value of AI in education is **personalization + automation + data insight**, and it must still protect **accuracy, fairness, and academic integrity**.

---

## 1. Why Is AI a Good Fit for Education?

Pain points in traditional online learning:

| Pain point | AI solution |
|---|---|
| Everyone moves at the same pace | Adaptive Learning |
| Students get stuck with no one to ask | AI Tutor 24/7 |
| Teachers get exhausted grading | Auto Grading |
| No visibility into who is falling behind | Learning Analytics |
| Content production is slow | Content Generation |

---

## 2. Business Models (Good Interview Material)

| Model | Customer | Value proposition |
|---|---|---|
| B2C | Students / parents | Better scores, study support, practice |
| B2B | Schools / institutions | Reduce teacher workload, measurable outcomes |
| B2B2C | Platforms + teachers | Tools that empower content creators |
| API / Platform | Other EdTech companies | Quiz-generation engine, speech-assessment API |

The ROI language executives love to hear:

> "Teacher grading time dropped by X%, student course completion rose by Y%, and dropout risk fell by Z% after early-warning intervention."

---

## 3. Reference Products (Use These for Interview Prep)

| Product | What to remember |
|---|---|
| **Khanmigo** | Socratic guidance; it does not hand out direct answers |
| **Duolingo** | Adaptive practice + gamification + AI exercises |
| **Coursera** | Large-scale courses + AI-assisted grading/content |
| **NotebookLM** | RAG for organizing study materials |
| **MagicSchool and similar tools** | Help teachers generate lesson plans and teaching materials |
| **Zuoyebang / Yuanfudao and similar companies** | Photo-based question search and a Chinese-market playbook |

Interview line:

> "What I especially agree with in Khanmigo's design is that AI should be a guide, not an answer machine. That matters for both transfer of learning and anti-cheating."

---

## 5. Non-Negotiable Red Lines (You Should Mention These)

1. **Teaching the wrong thing** - hallucinated content cannot go live without review  
2. **Cheating** - AI becomes a ghostwriting tool  
3. **Discrimination** - bias in grading and recommendation  
4. **Privacy** - minors' data and learning records  
5. **The "AI replaces teachers" narrative** - the industry dislikes it, and it is also wrong  

The right positioning:

> "AI amplifies the impact of great teachers and makes one-on-one experiences scalable."
