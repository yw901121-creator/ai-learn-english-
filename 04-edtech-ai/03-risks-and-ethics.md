# Chapter 4 | Risks, Ethics, and Anti-Cheating

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Education AI cannot afford to "teach the wrong thing" or become a cheating tool.  
Someone who can explain the risks clearly sounds far more credible than someone who only knows how to hype AI.

---

## 1. Four Major Risks

### A. Hallucination
AI teaches the wrong formula, date, or definition.  
**Mitigation:** RAG, citations, low-confidence refusal, teacher review.

### B. Academic Integrity (Cheating)
Students use AI to write assignments for them.  
**Mitigation:**
- Process-based assessment (drafts, oral exams, step-by-step scoring)
- AI-use policies (allowed for brainstorming, not allowed for final submission)
- TA mode that guides without giving the answer
- AI-generated-content detection when needed (not a silver bullet)

### C. Bias & Fairness
Scores or recommendations differ across groups.  
**Mitigation:** segmented evaluation, human sampling review, appeals mechanisms.

### D. Privacy
Minors' personal data and learning-behavior data.  
**Mitigation:** data minimization, permissions, retention limits, compliance (regulations vary by region).

---

## 2. Product Design: Practice Mode vs. Exam Mode

| | Practice mode | Exam mode |
|---|---|---|
| AI tutor | Guidance allowed | Usually disabled or reduced to the bare minimum |
| Level of hints | High | None |
| Goal | Learn | Measure fairly |

Mentioning this in an interview is a strong signal.

---

## 3. Division of Labor Between Teachers and AI

| AI does | Teacher does |
|---|---|
| Repetitive Q&A | Motivation and emotional support |
| First-pass review and analysis | Final grades and dispute handling |
| Draft content | Instructional design and value guidance |
| Early-warning lists | Real intervention and communication |

Strong line:

> "AI handles the 80% of repetitive questions; teachers focus on the 20% of moments that truly require a human."

---

## 4. If You Are Asked, "Will AI Replace Teachers?"

Standard answer:

> "It will not replace teachers, but it will reshape the work.  
> What gets replaced is the part that only repeats explanations and grades multiple-choice questions;  
> what becomes even more valuable is course design, guided thinking, and handling learning motivation.  
> Teachers who use AI well will become stronger, not disappear."

---

## Quick Quiz

1. What is the strategic difference between AI in practice mode and exam mode?  
2. For anti-cheating, is the better direction "catching" or "redesigning assessment"?  
3. Give one example of a privacy risk.

<details>
<summary>Suggested answers</summary>

1. Practice mode can guide; exam mode should be disabled or tightly restricted  
2. Redesigning assessment (process work, oral exams) is usually more effective than pure detection  
3. A minor's learning history leaking out, or data being used without parental consent  

</details>
