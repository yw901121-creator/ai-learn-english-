# Chapter 0 | Welcome and Study Mindset

## What do you need to achieve today?

When you interview at an "online education + AI" company, the hiring manager may ask, "How well do you understand AI?"  
You need to be able to **explain it clearly in 30 seconds, cover it fully in 2 minutes, and stay calm under follow-up questions**.

---

## The three layers interviewers are really listening for

| Level | Meaning | What you should demonstrate |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | You have heard the terms | LLM, RAG, Agent |
| **Understanding** | You can explain them in plain language | "RAG means looking up the information first, then answering." |
| **Application** | You can connect them to education scenarios | "Use RAG for an AI teaching assistant so it does not teach the wrong thing." |

If you only have the first layer -> it sounds like memorized vocabulary  
If you have the second layer -> you are qualified  
If you have the third layer -> **you gain an edge in hiring**

---

## The golden formula for this course

Whenever you explain any AI concept, use these four steps:

```
1. What is it? (one sentence)
2. Why is it needed? (the pain point)
3. How is it used? (an example)
4. What are its limits? (your judgment)
```

**Example: RAG**

1. **What is it**: Before answering, the AI first checks company or course materials  
2. **Why**: Model training data becomes outdated, and models can also make things up  
3. **How it is used**: A student asks, "What is the formula in Chapter 3?" and the AI retrieves the lecture notes before answering  
4. **Limit**: If the lecture notes do not contain it, the AI should not guess

Remember this formula and you can apply it throughout the whole course.

---

## How to position yourself in an interview

Do not say:

> "I know how to use ChatGPT."

Say:

> "I understand Generative AI, and I pay close attention to how **AI x education** is applied in practice — personalized learning, AI teaching assistants, and automated assessment. I know that accuracy and academic integrity are the bottom line in this field. AI is an accelerator for teachers, not a replacement."
